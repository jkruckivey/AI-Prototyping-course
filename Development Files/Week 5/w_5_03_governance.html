 Governance & Responsible AI

What this page covers

How to ship models safely and credibly: risk tiers, approvals, documentation, privacy controls, explainability, fairness reviews, and change management. You’ll leave with a lightweight governance checklist you can adapt to your org.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Classify models by risk tier and route them through the right level of review.
  2. Document a model with purpose, data, metrics, limitations, and guardrails.
  3. Set up privacy, fairness, and change‑control practices that stand up to audits.

 Risk Tiers & Approvals

Define tiers

  • T1 — Low: internal analytics, no automated actions, low impact.
  • T2 — Medium: customer‑facing recommendations, human‑in‑the‑loop, moderate impact.
  • T3 — High: automated decisions that affect access/eligibility, safety, or financial outcomes.

Higher tiers need stricter validation, fairness checks, legal/ethics review, and executive sign‑off.

Ownership (RACI)

Clarify who is Responsible (build/monitor), Accountable (business owner), Consulted (legal, risk, privacy), and Informed (ops, exec sponsors).

 Documentation & Transparency

Model card essentials

  • Purpose & scope (in/out of scope use).
  • Data (sources, time span, sampling, known gaps).
  • Metrics (overall & by segment; calibration).
  • Limitations & ethical risks.
  • Operational guardrails (thresholds, review queues, rate limits).
  • Monitoring plan (KPIs, drift tests, alert rules, owners).

Explainability

Use global feature importance for trends and local explanations for case decisions (e.g., top contributing factors). Avoid exposing sensitive attributes or inferring them.

 Privacy & Data Protection

Core controls

  • Minimize data; collect only what’s necessary for the stated purpose.
  • Identify PII/sensitive attributes; apply protection (encryption at rest/in transit, access controls).
  • Define retention & deletion policies; log data lineage and access.

Consent & disclosure

State what is automated, how data is used, and how to seek human review or appeal, consistent with local regulations and company policy.

 Fairness Reviews

Subgroup metrics

Report precision/recall, approval rates, and calibration by subgroup. Investigate large gaps; document mitigations (thresholds by segment, features review, additional oversight).

Impact assessment

Conduct a lightweight impact assessment: populations affected, failure modes, reversibility, compensation/appeal process, and communication plan.

 Change Control & Auditability

Versioning & approvals

  • Version data, code, and models; tag deployments with model + config.
  • Require approvals for T2/T3 changes; document rationale and expected impact.
  • Keep a changelog and link to evaluation reports.

Incidents & post‑mortems

Define severity levels, on‑call rotation, rollback triggers, and post‑mortem templates with action items and owners.
