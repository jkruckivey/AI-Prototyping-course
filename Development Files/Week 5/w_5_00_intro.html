 Week 5 — Orientation

What this week is about

This week moves from modeling to making models work in the real world. You’ll connect deployment choices to business workflows, design monitoring for model health and fairness, and outline governance that keeps systems reliable over time.

 Before you start

Readiness checklist

  • You can map a business problem to a supervised learning task (classification vs regression).
  • You’ve used the right metrics (precision/recall, MAE/RMSE) and understand thresholds.
  • You’re familiar with cross‑validation, leak‑safe pipelines, and early stopping.

Need a refresher? See: Metrics & Model Fit · Cross‑Validation · Business Lens on Metrics

What you'll do this week

  • Sketch deployment options and last‑mile integration (APIs, batch jobs, human‑in‑the‑loop).
  • Design monitoring: data & concept drift, calibration, fairness checks, business KPIs.
  • Outline governance: owners, SLAs, change control, incident response, documentation.

 Warm‑up: The Last‑Mile Gap

Reflect: What could stop a working model from delivering business value after it leaves the notebook? List technical and organizational blockers.

 How to get the most out of Week 5

  • Design for decisions: Start from the action you want (block, flag, route, message) and work backward.
  • Instrument everything: Log inputs, scores, decisions, and outcomes to power monitoring and audits.
  • Plan ownership: Define who responds to drift, outages, and fairness issues—and within what SLA.
