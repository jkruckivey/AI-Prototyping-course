 Knowledge Check

1) Which algorithm directly minimizes MSE to fit numeric targets?

Linear Regression ✓ — fits a line/plane by minimizing mean squared error.

2) In logistic regression, what does the decision threshold control?

It converts predicted probabilities into class labels, trading precision vs. recall based on business cost.

3) Why is feature scaling important for KNN and SVM?

Because both depend on distances; unscaled features can dominate and distort neighborhoods/margins.

4) Random Forests vs. a single Decision Tree — what’s the typical advantage?

Lower variance and better generalization via bagging and feature subsampling; often stronger accuracy.

5) Naive Bayes works well for text mainly because…

With sparse, high‑dimensional features, the independence assumption is a decent approximation and the model is fast and effective.

6) Neural networks add value when…

Data is large/complex, patterns are non‑linear, and simple baselines plateau; NNs can learn feature representations (e.g., images, audio).

 Mini Case: Rare‑Event Churn

Scenario

Your dataset has 1% churners. Sketch your first experiments: which models will you try, how will you handle imbalance, and which metrics will you track?

  • Start baselines: logistic regression, tree, maybe Naive Bayes (text‑ish features).
  • Consider class weights / resampling; evaluate with precision/recall & PR curves.
  • Tie the threshold to business cost (FP vs FN).

Helpful Tip

Always benchmark against a simple baseline (e.g., logistic regression). Complexity should earn its keep with measurable lift.
