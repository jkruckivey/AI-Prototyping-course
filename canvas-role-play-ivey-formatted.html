 AI Implementation Role-Play: "The Digital Transformation Board Meeting"

Executive simulation where you'll make strategic AI investment decisions within budget and timeline constraints.

Learning Objectives
  • Develop: Executive decision-making skills for AI implementation strategy
  • Create: Compelling business cases that demonstrate measurable ROI
  • Design: Comprehensive data strategies that enable AI success
  • Build: Ethical frameworks for responsible AI deployment
  • Practice: Collaborative problem-solving in complex business scenarios

 Student Instructions & Setup

Complete these preparation steps before the role-play meeting begins.

Phase 1: Pre-Meeting Preparation (10 minutes)

Step 1: Role Assignment and Preparation

Your Executive Role: You will be assigned one of six critical positions:

  • Chief Executive Officer (CEO) - Strategic vision and board accountability
  • Chief Financial Officer (CFO) - Budget oversight and ROI justification
  • Chief Operating Officer (COO) - Operational efficiency and implementation
  • Chief Technology Officer (CTO) - Technical feasibility and infrastructure
  • Head of Sales - Customer impact and revenue generation
  • Head of Human Resources - Workforce transformation and change management

Preparation Tasks:

  • Review your role's specific objectives, concerns, and success metrics
  • Consider how your functional perspective shapes AI investment priorities
  • Prepare 2-3 key questions or concerns to raise during the meeting
  • Think about potential conflicts with other departments' priorities
Step 2: Company Context Review

MidMarket Manufacturing Inc. - Current Situation:

  • Budget Constraint: $2.5 million allocated for AI initiatives over 18 months
  • Competitive Pressure: 12% market share decline as competitors leverage AI advantages
  • Cost Challenge: Manufacturing costs 18% higher than AI-enabled competitors
  • Customer Requirements: Key clients now requesting AI-powered capabilities for contract renewals
  • Strategic Assets: 15 years of operational data, strong customer relationships, experienced workforce

Key Question: How can we leverage our data and relationships while closing the AI capability gap within our budget and timeline constraints?

 Meeting Structure & Guidelines

The role-play meeting follows a structured 45-minute format with three distinct phases.

Phase 2: Meeting Execution (45 minutes)
  • Situation Assessment (15 minutes)
    • Present role-specific business challenges
    • Identify AI opportunities for your functional area
    • Listen actively and note potential conflicts
  • Strategy Development (20 minutes)
    • Collaborate to evaluate AI investment options
    • Build consensus around priority initiatives
    • Address resource allocation and timelines
  • Decision Making (10 minutes)
    • Finalize AI investment strategy and budget allocation
    • Assign responsibilities and success metrics
    • Prepare brief presentation of final decisions
⚠️ Scenario Complications

Note: Expect unexpected challenges to be introduced around minute 25, including:

  • Budget constraints or competing priorities
  • Technical limitations or vendor issues
  • Regulatory or compliance concerns
  • Market changes or competitive responses

Your team must adapt your strategy while maintaining focus on measurable business outcomes.

 Role-Play Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to ensure authentic and productive executive-level decision-making.

Collaboration Framework

Executive Mindset Guidelines
  • Stay In Character: Advocate authentically for your functional priorities
  • Think Like an Executive: Focus on business impact, not technical features
  • Collaborate Effectively: Listen first, then build on others' ideas
  • Make Realistic Decisions: Work within $2.5M budget and 18-month timeline

 Executive AI Learning Activities

Complete these three integrated activities to develop comprehensive AI leadership capabilities.

Activity Overview
  • Total Time Investment: 60-75 minutes across all activities
  • Learning Progression: Business Case → Data Strategy → Ethics Framework
  • Final Outcome: Complete AI implementation roadmap ready for executive presentation

 Activity 1: AI Business Case Builder

Create a compelling, data-driven business case for AI adoption using hands-on experience with Google's Teachable Machine.

🎯 Learning Objective

Create a compelling, data-driven business case for AI adoption that executives can apply directly to their organizations. This activity provides hands-on experience building a simple AI system using Google's Teachable Machine and translating that experience into ROI-focused strategy.

Executives often understand AI conceptually but struggle to turn that into actionable business cases. By experimenting with AI model training and testing, participants gain insight into data requirements, performance limitations, and business value.

Phase 1: AI System Development and Testing (20 minutes)

Technical Implementation Steps
  • Minutes 1-5: Navigate to Teachable Machine, select Image Project, and choose a business scenario:
    • Quality Control: Defect detection in manufacturing
    • Customer Service: Document classification or sentiment analysis
    • Inventory Management: Product categorization or counting
    • Safety Compliance: Equipment or behavior monitoring
  • Minutes 6-15: Upload 10-15 labeled examples per category, train your model, and observe:
    • How data variety and quality impact accuracy
    • Training time and computational requirements
    • Model confidence levels and uncertainty indicators
  • Minutes 16-20: Test the trained model with new images, noting:
    • Confidence levels and prediction accuracy
    • Edge cases where human oversight remains necessary
    • Potential failure modes and business risks

Phase 2: Business Impact Analysis and ROI Calculation (15 minutes)

💰 Financial Analysis Framework

Use this structured approach to calculate business value:

  • Annual Volume: How many items/transactions per year? (e.g., 50,000 quality inspections)
  • Current Costs: Labor, errors, delays, rework costs? (e.g., $200,000 annual inspection costs)
  • Error Rate Impact: Cost of false positives and negatives? (e.g., $50,000 in defect-related costs)
  • AI Efficiency Gains: Time savings and accuracy improvements? (e.g., 40% faster, 85% accuracy)
  • Implementation Costs: Software, training, integration expenses? (e.g., $75,000 setup + $15,000 annual)

Phase 3: Executive Summary Creation (10 minutes)

📋 Executive Summary Requirements

Format: 1-2 page executive summary (or 3-4 slides)

Target Audience: C-suite executives and board members

  • Problem Definition: Clear business challenge with quantified impact
  • AI Solution Approach: Specific technology recommendations and methodology
  • Implementation Requirements: Resources, timeline, and organizational changes needed
  • ROI and Strategic Value: Financial projections and competitive advantages
  • Risk Assessment: Technical, operational, and business risks with mitigation strategies
  • Success Metrics: Measurable KPIs and evaluation framework

Success Criteria: Your business case should convince a CFO to approve funding by demonstrating measurable financial impact, realistic implementation approach, and comprehensive risk management.

📤 Deliverables
  • Primary: Executive summary document (Word/PDF or PowerPoint slides)
  • Optional: Link to your Teachable Machine project for demonstration
  • Bonus: Implementation timeline with key milestones and dependencies

 Activity 2: Data Strategy Workshop

Develop actionable data strategy using real datasets that moves from conceptual principles to practical business insight generation.

🎯 Learning Objective

Develop actionable data strategy using real datasets that moves learners from conceptual data principles to practical business insight generation. This activity demonstrates how data quality, infrastructure, and governance directly impact AI implementation success.

Phase 1: Dataset Exploration (15 minutes)

📈 Industry Dataset Options
  • Retail Sales: Customer demographics, purchase history, seasonality
    Applications: Customer segmentation, demand forecasting, inventory optimization
  • Manufacturing Operations: Equipment performance, quality metrics, downtime data
    Applications: Predictive maintenance, quality control, efficiency optimization
  • Financial Services: Transaction patterns, risk indicators, customer behavior
    Applications: Fraud detection, credit scoring, personalized services
  • Healthcare Operations: Patient outcomes, resource utilization, treatment effectiveness
    Applications: Care optimization, resource planning, outcome prediction

Phase 2: Insight Development (10 minutes)

💡 Business Insight Framework

Identify three actionable insights from your data analysis:

  • Pattern Description: What did you discover in the data?
  • Business Impact: How does this affect operations or outcomes?
  • Action Opportunity: What specific steps could address this pattern?
  • Success Metrics: How would you measure improvement?

Example: "Customers who purchase products X and Y together have 40% higher lifetime value and 60% lower churn rates. Recommendation: Implement bundling strategy and targeted cross-selling campaigns."

Phase 3: Strategic Recommendations (10 minutes)

🎯 Implementation Planning Components
  • Data Infrastructure: Storage, integration, security, accessibility
  • Technology Requirements: AI/ML platforms, analytics tools, integration systems
  • Organizational Changes: Skills development, process redesign, governance
  • Financial Investment: Initial costs, ongoing expenses, ROI timeline

Phase 4: Executive Summary (10 minutes)

📋 Executive Communication Requirements

Create a concise memo or slide deck that executives can act upon:

  • Executive Summary: Key insights and recommendations in 3-4 sentences
  • Data Analysis Results: Top 3 insights with business impact quantification
  • Strategic Recommendations: Prioritized action items with timelines
  • Implementation Plan: Resources, phases, and success metrics
  • ROI Projections: Expected returns and payback period
  • Risk Assessment: Key challenges and mitigation strategies
📤 Deliverables
  • Primary: Executive memo or slide deck (Word/PDF or PowerPoint)
  • Optional: Link to Google Sheet with analysis if conducted online
  • Bonus: Implementation timeline with key milestones and dependencies

 Activity 3: AI Ethics and Risk Assessment

Evaluate ethical and risk dimensions of AI adoption through scenario-based planning that balances business goals with stakeholder protection.

🎯 Learning Objective

Evaluate ethical and risk dimensions of AI adoption through scenario-based planning. This activity builds frameworks for responsible AI governance that balance business goals with stakeholder protection, ensuring sustainable and trustworthy AI implementation.

Phase 1: Scenario Analysis (10 minutes)

🎭 Ethical Scenario Selection

Choose one complex scenario to analyze in depth:

Scenario A: Hiring Algorithm Bias

Situation: Your AI hiring system increases efficiency by 60% and reduces time-to-hire from 45 to 18 days. However, analysis reveals it systematically favors candidates from certain universities and demographic groups, potentially perpetuating existing workplace inequities.

Key Tensions: Operational efficiency vs. fairness and diversity

Stakeholders: HR teams, candidates, current employees, legal/compliance, executives, shareholders

Scenario B: Customer Privacy vs. Personalization

Situation: Your AI personalization engine increases customer satisfaction by 35% and revenue by 20% through sophisticated behavioral prediction. However, it requires extensive personal data collection that customers don't fully understand, raising privacy and consent concerns.

Key Tensions: Business growth vs. customer privacy rights

Stakeholders: Customers, marketing teams, data scientists, legal/compliance, privacy advocates, regulators

Scenario C: Workforce Automation Impact

Situation: Your AI automation system can eliminate 40% of current positions while improving accuracy and reducing costs by $2M annually. However, this affects long-term employees and could damage community relationships and company culture.

Key Tensions: Cost reduction vs. employee welfare

Stakeholders: Affected employees, remaining workforce, unions, local community, customers, shareholders

Phase 2: Framework Development (5 minutes)

🛡️ Governance Framework Components
  • Ethical Safeguards: Bias detection, fairness metrics, transparency standards
  • Monitoring Requirements: Performance tracking, outcome measurement, stakeholder feedback
  • Escalation Procedures: Issue identification, response protocols, decision authority
  • Stakeholder Protection: Rights preservation, harm prevention, remedy processes

Phase 3: Executive Brief (5 minutes)

📋 Executive Brief Requirements

Create a concise 1-page brief that provides clear guidance for executive decision-making:

  • Scenario Summary: Brief description of the ethical challenge and key stakeholders
  • Risk Analysis: Primary risks to business, stakeholders, and reputation
  • Governance Recommendations: Specific policies and procedures to implement
  • Business Implications: Impact on operations, costs, and competitive position
  • Implementation Plan: Steps to deploy ethical framework with timelines
  • Success Metrics: How to measure responsible AI performance

Success Criteria: Your ethics framework should protect all stakeholders while enabling innovation, demonstrating that responsible AI practices strengthen rather than constrain business outcomes.

📤 Deliverables
  • Primary: Executive brief document (Word/PDF, 1-page maximum)
  • Required Elements: Scenario analysis, governance framework, implementation recommendations
  • Bonus: Policy template or checklist for ongoing ethical AI governance
