The Accuracy Trap

A model that predicts "no fraud" for every transaction would have 99.9% accuracy! Here's why:

  • 99.9% of transactions are legitimate â†’ Always predicting "no fraud" gets 99.9% right
  • But this model catches 0% of actual fraud
  • All fraudulent transactions slip through undetected

Better Metrics for This Context

  • Precision: Of flagged transactions, how many are actually fraud?
  • Recall: Of actual fraud, how much did we catch?
  • Cost-based evaluation: Expected savings vs. review costs
  • Lift: How much better than random selection?

Business Impact

The right metric depends on business priorities:

  • High precision: Minimize false alarms (staff efficiency)
  • High recall: Catch more fraud (reduce losses)
  • Balanced approach: Optimize total expected value
